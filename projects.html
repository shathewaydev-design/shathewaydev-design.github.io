Puzzles, Politeness, and Tea

An academic paper where I explore the game series Professor Layton, its relationship between the ideals of a Soft-Europe and how it is utilized to create a vessel for Japanese societal values, as well as how these values are shared across the world.

When writing this paper, I didn't just want to gloat about the simple yet admirable game design put into Professor Layton that makes it so alluring, but dig deeper into why certain decisions were made within the development process. The game is hidden behind a veil of European aesthetics and characters, but at its core it reflects nostalgia, civility, and educational morality that are rooted in Japanese culture. This paper explores how, why, and the importance of these values.

  • Paper: Puzzles, Politeness, and Tea: Nostalgia, Civility, and Educational Morality as Reflections of Japanese Ideals and Philosophy in Professor Layton amid Globalization
  • Duration: 3 Weeks
  • Team Size: 1
